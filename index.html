INFO: What is Fermentation?

Fermentation is a chemical process, where molecules, glucose, bacteria, yeast are broken down. Oxygen is needed for many living organisms, but in this process oxygen is excluded. Many types of fermented food that we eat are cheese, yogurt, sourdoughbread, kimchi, sauerkraut, the list goes on.

There are 3 main types of Fermentation

Lactic Acid Fermentation, Ethyl Alcohol Fermentation, Acetic Acid Fermentation

Compounds and Molecules in the Fermentation process

Glucose: a simple sugar with the molecular formula C₆H₁₂O₆

ADP/Adenosine Diphosphate: an organic compound that transfers energy to cells

ATP/Adenosine Triphosphate: stores and transfers energy

NAD+: coenzyme that is important to metabolism

NADH: co-enzyme required for the production of energy in cells important to metabolism

Pyruvate: it is the output of the metabolism of glucose known as glycolysis

Benefits of Fermentation

Eating fermented foods improves the digestive system and immune system. Fermentation increases the nutritional value of raw produce. It can create desirable tastes and textures. If people eat probiotics (like those found in fermented foods) from early childhood, that can help train the immune system to tolerate — and cooperate with — a diverse, beneficial microbiome.
