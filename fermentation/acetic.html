Acetic Acid Fermentation

Chemical Formula: C2H5OH + O2 → CH3COOH + H2O

Acetic Acid Fermentation is produced from the oxidation of Ethanol Fermentation or Alcohol Fermentation. It’s mainly used to make vinegar.

The fermentation process is built off of the same process as Ethanol Fermentation or Alcohol Fermentation, the end result with Acetic Acid molecules.

Process: Glucose is converted into 2 Pyruvate molecules, while 2 ADP is converted into 2 ATP, and 2 NAD+ is converted into 2 NADH. The 2 Pyruvate molecules is converted into 2 Acetaldehyde molecules. The 2 Acetaldehyde molecules is converted into 2 Ethanol molecules. The 2 Ethanol molecules is converted to Acetic Acid molecules, and the 2 NADH is converted back into 2 NAD+
