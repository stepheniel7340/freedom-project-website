Lactic Acid Fermentation

Chemical Formula: C₆H₁₂O₆ —> 2 CH₃CHCOOH

Lactic Acid Fermentation occurs from the absence of oxygen, through glycolysis. It’s a metabolic process where glucose is converted into energy. The pyruvate molecules from glucose metabolism (glycolysis) is fermented into lactic acid.

List of Lactic Acids foods: Yogurt, Cheese, Pickles, Saurkraut, Sourdough Bread, and much more

Process: Glucose is converted into 2 Pyruvate molecules, while 2 ADP is converted into 2 ATP, and 2 NAD+ is converted into 2 NADH. Finally, the 2 Pyruvate molecules is converted into 2 Lactate molecules, and the 2 NADH is converted back into 2 NAD+
