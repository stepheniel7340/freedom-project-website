Ethanol Fermentation/Alcohol Fermentation

Chemical Formula: C₆H₁₂O₆ —> 2 C2H5OH + 2 CO2

Ethanol Fermentation or Alcohol Fermentation is a process where sugars are converted into cellular energy to make ethanol. Yeast converts pyruvate to carbon dioxide and ethanol.

The process is used to make mainly alcoholic beverages like: Beer, Wine, Vodka, Whiskey, and much more

Process: Glucose is converted into 2 Pyruvate molecules, while 2 ADP is converted into 2 ATP, and 2 NAD+ is converted into 2 NADH. The 2 Pyruvate molecules is converted into 2 Acetaldehyde molecules. The 2 Acetaldehyde molecules is converted into 2 Ethanol molecules, and the 2 NADH is converted back into 2 NAD+
